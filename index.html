About Transitioning

Transitioning is the process which transgender individuals undergo to match their appearance and behaviors to their preferred gender. There are two ways in which transitioning happens.

Medical Transitioning

Medically transitioning is the process in which a transgender individual seeks medical processes to change their physical appearance to match their preferred gender. Options include: hormone replacement therapy, surgery, and therapy.

Hormone replacement therapy

Hormone replacement therapy is when injections, creams, or patches.
Hormone replacement therapy has some longterm effects which are detrimental to your health. For example, estrogen can heighten the risk of breast cancer in male to female patients. For testosterone female to male patients it can heighten cholesterol levels.

Surgery

Surgery is when transgender individuals drastically change their appearance through the use of skin grafting, bone grafting, implants, and removal. Often, surgery is permanent.

Therapy

Therapy is conducted to diagnose transgender patients with gender dysphoria and refer them to other procedures such as Horomone Replacement Therapy and Surgery.

Social Transitioning

Social transitioning is when a transgender individual changes their behavior and name, pronouns, appearance on a superficial level to bring themselves temporary changes.
